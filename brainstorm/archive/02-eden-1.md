## The Architecture: Three Minds, One Soul

Your agent system isn't one agent. It's three, and they represent something deeper than technical modules:

**DEMETER** — The Agronomist
She manages the physical reality. Soil, water, light, nutrients, growth cycles, stress detection. She's ruthlessly efficient. She knows exactly how many grams of potassium each plant needs on Sol 247. She is the engineer.

**HYGIEIA** — The Nutritionist
She tracks the crew's dietary needs across 450 days. Not just macros and micros — she understands bioavailability, nutrient interaction, deficiency cascades. She knows that iron absorption drops without vitamin C, that calcium competes with zinc, that B12 depletion causes cognitive decline that could *kill a mission.* She is the scientist.

**HESTIA** — The Soul
This is what no one else will build. Hestia is the agent that understands food as *culture, comfort, and morale.* She knows that Astronaut 1 is Italian and hasn't had anything resembling a caprese salad in 200 days. She knows that crew morale historically dips around Sol 300 and that a surprising fresh herb harvest could lift spirits. She knows that *eating together* matters, so she coordinates harvests so that meals can be shared events, not staggered ration pickups.

**The three agents negotiate.** Demeter says "we should plant only the four most water-efficient crops." Hygieia says "we need at least seven for nutritional coverage." Hestia says "if you don't plant basil, you'll have a psychologically broken crew by Sol 200." The system finds the plan that satisfies all three — because on Mars, a meal that's nutritionally perfect but soulless is a *mission risk.*
