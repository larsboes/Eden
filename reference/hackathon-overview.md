# **Syngenta x AWS Hackathon: Martian Greenhouse AI Agent**

**Event:** START HACK 2026 | **Challenge:** Agriculture on Mars

## **Slide 1: The Vision & Context**

**Main Text:**

* NASA is targeting the late 2030s to send humans to Mars, and with a trip that could take up to nine months each way, figuring out how to grow food on the Red Planet is a critical mission.  
* Meanwhile on Earth, Claude AI is already being used to grow plants fully autonomously.  
* Our challenge combines these elements: To design an autonomous AI agent system to manage a Martian greenhouse.

**Visual Context:**

An astronaut in a full spacesuit kneeling on red Martian soil, examining a young, green crop (looks like corn/maize) using a glowing, futuristic digital tablet.

**Strategic Takeaway:** The narrative combines deep space exploration with bleeding-edge LLM capabilities (specifically calling out Claude AI).

## **Slide 2: The Earthly Problem (Global Context)**

**Main Text:**

* By 2050, food production around the world must rise by half to sustain a global population.  
* Last 70 Years: Agriculture has fed a further 5bn people on almost the same amount of land.  
* Next 30 Years: \+2bn people, \+50% more food needed.

**Visual Context:**

A split graphic shaped like an arrow. The left side shows a yellow tractor tilling a massive red field from a top-down view. The right side shows a dense crowd of people in a city.

**Strategic Takeaway:** While the hackathon is Mars-themed, the underlying goal is to create high-efficiency, autonomous farming tech that can solve Earth's impending food crisis. Mentioning Earth applications in your pitch will be a strong move.

## **Slide 3: The Core Challenge**

**Main Text:**

* **Our Challenge:** To design an autonomous AI agent system to manage a Martian greenhouse.  
* Your mission, should you choose to accept it, is to design and prototype an autonomous AI agent system capable of managing a greenhouse on Mars.  
* **Your system should be able to:**  
  * *Monitor and Control the Environment:* Maintain optimal conditions for plant growth (temperature, humidity, light, water).  
  * *Manage Resources:* Efficiently use and recycle precious resources like water and nutrients.  
  * *Detect and Respond to Plant Stress:* Identify plant health issues (e.g., nutrient deficiencies, disease) and trigger automated responses.  
  * *Optimize for Growth:* Learn and adapt to find the most effective strategies for growing crops in an alien environment.

**Visual Context:**

A webcam screenshot (looks like a time-lapse or monitoring feed) of a real plant (possibly a cucumber or tomato vine) growing inside a reflective, indoor grow tent under artificial lights.

**Strategic Takeaway:** This is your feature roadmap. Your Digital Twin / K8s-style orchestration must demonstrate these four specific capabilities.

## **Slide 4: Architecture & Latency (Earth vs. Mars)**

**Main Text:**

* **EARTH – Far Away** vs. **MARS – Focal Point**  
* **Distance:** 401 MILLION KILOMETERS (Real-time remote support & expert advice).  
* **AWS CLOUD (Earth):** AWS Region (e.g. US-East-1) \-\> Syngenta account \-\> Expert Ag-Tech Knowledge \+ Farming use case data \-\> AWS Data Store \-\> AWS AI Platform (Amazon Bedrock AgenCore / Agent AI Services).  
* *Data loops:* "Syngenta guide to the Mars Farming" and "Syngenta guide to the Galaxy digital companion" pass between Earth and Mars via a Remote support interface.  
* **Mars Dialogue Bubble:** "Why is my plant sick? Need to increase yield for 4 astronauts for 450 days\!"

**Visual Context:**

A highly detailed diagram. On the left is Earth in space. On the right is a futuristic transparent dome greenhouse on Mars. Inside, an astronaut stands next to a large central plant, surrounded by server racks, robotic arms, and glowing hydroponic tubes. An architecture diagram bridges the two planets showing data flow.

**Strategic Takeaway:** You cannot rely on real-time API calls to Earth for critical split-second decisions. Your AI agent needs to act locally (Edge AI) but perhaps syncs with Earth for long-term "expert" knowledge.

## **Slide 5: Mission Parameters & Deliverables**

**Main Text:**

* **Mission parameters:**  
  * Crew size: 4 astronauts  
  * Duration: 450-day mission on Mars  
  * Goal: Maximize nutrient output \+ balanced diet  
  * Constraints: minimal resource use, minimal astronaut time  
* **System requirements:**  
  * Maintain greenhouse environment (temp, humidity, light, water)  
  * Manage and recycle resources  
  * Detect plant stress & respond automatically  
  * Optimize crop growth and adapt over time  
* **Expected outputs:**  
  * *Proof of Concept:* A working simulation or digital twin of your Martian greenhouse demonstrating your AI agent in action.  
  * *A 3-Minute PowerPoint Presentation:* A concise pitch of your solution, outlining the architecture of your AI agent system, the key features, and why your approach is innovative and feasible.  
  * *Crucial Note:* There must be a working agent system behind the PoC.

**Visual Context:**

Text is laid out in three rounded boxes. The background shows a photo of hackathon participants (a team) looking at a project, establishing the hacking environment.

**Strategic Takeaway:** Do not fake the AI backend. They explicitly state "There must be a working agent system behind the PoC." Your simulation needs to feed actual data to an LLM/Agent, which then returns actual commands.
