# EDEN — Final Pitch V2 (5 minutes)

> Integrates: original pitch strengths (plant proof, live demo, Architect concept) + CTO intel (food psychology, Earth mapping, general audience) + 14-agent adversarial testing (silence moment, named specificity, predictive capability). Written for spoken delivery. Read it out loud.

---

## BEFORE YOU START

- Plant on table, LEDs warm white, fan running softly
- Dashboard live on screen
- One person speaks. One person drives. One on prop. One on backup.
- Speaker faces judges at all times during key moments.

---

## ACT 1: THE PROOF (0:00–0:30)

*"See this plant? Nobody has touched it in 24 hours. No human watered it. No human adjusted the light. No human checked the soil. An AI did all of that — autonomously — while we slept."*

**Pause. Let them look at it.**

*"That's not a simulation. That's a real plant, with real sensors, managed by a real autonomous agent system running on AWS right now. Every decision it made is logged. Every reading is live."*

**Gesture to dashboard.**

*"We were asked to design an autonomous AI agent system to manage a greenhouse on Mars. We didn't just design one. We built one. And it's running on this table. We call it EDEN."*

**~80 words. ~30 seconds.**

---

## ACT 2: WHY MARS IS HARD (0:30–1:15)

*"Let's talk about why Mars is hard — and it's not what you think."*

*"We know how to grow plants. Hydroponics, controlled environments, LED lighting — solved problems. Monitoring sensors and adjusting water — any good team can automate that."*

*"What makes Mars different is one thing: there is no backup. On Earth, a bad harvest means you import food. A broken pump means you drive to the store. On Mars, none of that exists. What you brought is what you have. What you grow is what you eat. Every liter of water, every watt of energy cycles through a closed loop — and if the system fails, there is no plan B. Just four astronauts and 450 days."*

*"And here's what most people miss: after 60 days of freeze-dried meals, food stops being food. NASA classifies fresh produce as a psychological countermeasure — same category as exercise. That greenhouse isn't a luxury. It's the only variety, the only freshness, the only thing that still feels normal. It's mental health infrastructure."*

*"So the question isn't 'can we grow plants.' It's: can we build an AI that manages an entire closed-loop food system — monitoring, resources, stress response, and continuous learning — with zero human intervention, zero margin for error, and an understanding that food isn't just fuel? It's how people stay human."*

*"That's what EDEN does."*

**~190 words. ~45 seconds.**

---

## ACT 3: THE SYSTEM (1:15–2:00)

*"EDEN is built on a principle from infrastructure engineering. If any engineers in the room know Kubernetes — this is Kubernetes for farming. But you don't need to know that. What matters is: every plant is tracked individually, resources are balanced automatically, and if something fails, the system heals itself."*

*"Running this system: five AI agents, operating as peers — not a hierarchy, because on Mars with a 22-minute communication delay, you can't afford a single point of failure."*

**Point to the agent panel on dashboard.**

*"The MARS Agent monitors the external environment — radiation, dust, solar. The CREW Agent tracks nutritional AND psychological needs — it knows that Commander Okonkwo needs iron on Sol 300, and it knows the crew hasn't had fresh greens in three weeks. The GREENHOUSE Agent manages every plant, every zone, every growth cycle. The RESOURCE Agent runs the circular economy — water, energy, nutrients cycling in closed loops. And the ORCHESTRATOR convenes the others when a decision cuts across domains."*

*"Each agent has specialized tools, including access to Syngenta's crop science knowledge base — the same data agronomists use on Earth. And when the system needs human hands — a physical repair, a seed change — any agent can call rent-a-human: a formal request to an astronaut with the task, priority, time estimate, and reasoning."*

**~195 words. ~45 seconds.**

---

## ACT 4: THE LIVE DEMO (2:00–3:00)

*"Now let me show you EDEN doing the hardest thing in the brief: detecting and responding to plant stress. Live. On a real plant."*

**Walk to the plant. Block the light sensor or alter the environment.**

*"I just cut the light to our plant. Watch the dashboard."*

**Pause 3-4 seconds. Let the sensors update. Let the audience see it change.**

*"The Greenhouse Agent detected it instantly. First thing: it checks the Flight Rules — a library of deterministic response protocols. Known situation, instant response, zero latency, no AI reasoning needed."*

**Show flight rule firing on dashboard.**

*"Light deficit detected. Compensate with supplemental LED. Fires in under a second. The plant is already getting light again."*

*"But here's what happens underneath. The system doesn't just react — it thinks. The Resource Agent checks: do we have the energy budget for this, or does it compete with another zone? The Crew Agent checks: is this crop on a critical nutritional or morale timeline? The agents share data, assess tradeoffs, and confirm or adjust."*

**3 SECONDS OF SILENCE. Let the dashboard work. Let the agents reason. Let judges watch.**

*"And tonight — the Dream Engine logs this event and runs thousands of simulated variants. What if this happens in Zone A instead? What if it coincides with a dust storm? From those simulations, EDEN writes NEW flight rules — so next time, the response is faster and smarter."*

*"That's the full loop: Monitor. Detect. Respond. Learn. All four requirements in the brief — working, right now, on this table."*

**Restore the light. Let readings normalize.**

*"Back to nominal. Autonomously."*

**~210 words + pauses. ~60 seconds.**

---

## ACT 5: PREDICTION (3:00–3:20)

*"What you just saw was reactive — something happened, EDEN responded. But EDEN also looks ahead."*

*"Right now, based on seasonal data and Mars orbital mechanics, EDEN is projecting a dust storm window in 47 sols. A major storm can cut solar power to 30 percent for weeks. That means less light, less water production, less heating."*

*"EDEN is already preparing. Pre-harvesting crops that won't survive reduced light. Shifting nutrient concentrations to harden the rest. Stockpiling water while the sun still shines. The crisis hasn't happened. EDEN is 47 days ahead of it."*

**~90 words. ~20 seconds.**

---

## ACT 6: THE ARCHITECT (3:20–3:50)

*"Every team today will show you a system that runs a greenhouse. EDEN does something none of them do: it designs the greenhouse first."*

*"Before a single seed is planted, you give EDEN your constraints: four astronauts, 450 days, available cargo mass, seed inventory, Mars conditions. EDEN outputs a complete system design — which crops, how many, which zones, a full 450-day planting and harvest schedule, and projected nutritional coverage for every crew member, every week."*

**Flash the Architect output — the timeline, zone layout, nutrition projections.**

*"It plans backward from human needs. It knows spinach has to go in the ground on Sol 250 so there's iron available on Sol 300. It staggers plantings so there's always something fresh to harvest. Every agricultural AI starts with a farm that already exists. EDEN starts with constraints and creates the farm."*

**~130 words. ~30 seconds.**

---

## ACT 7: THE EARTH BRIDGE (3:50–4:30)

*"We built EDEN for Mars because Mars is the ultimate test: zero redundancy, complete closure, no margin for error."*

*"But here's the truth — that description is starting to apply to Earth."*

*"Aquifers depleting. Soil degrading. Climate making growing conditions unpredictable. Farmers losing the redundancy that agriculture has always depended on."*

*"Now imagine Grace — a smallholder farmer in Nakuru, Kenya. Two hectares. Seasonal rainfall. Family of six. She inputs her constraints. EDEN designs her optimal farm. Same crop science from Syngenta's knowledge base. Same intelligence. Same closed-loop thinking."*

*"A vertical farm operator has a warehouse and an energy budget. Same problem. A humanitarian organization needs to feed a refugee camp for 6 months with limited water. Same problem."*

*"Syngenta's mission is bringing plant potential to life across 100 countries. The next frontier isn't just better seeds or better sensors. It's an intelligence layer that can design, operate, and evolve complete food systems — under any constraints, on any planet."*

**~155 words. ~40 seconds.**

---

## ACT 8: THE CLOSE (4:30–4:50)

*"We were asked to build an autonomous AI agent system to manage a Martian greenhouse."*

*"We built one that designs it, runs it, and teaches itself."*

*"We proved it on the hardest farm in the solar system."*

**Look at the judges. Not the screen.**

*"And we proved it on that plant."*

**Gesture to the basil. LEDs full warm white. Fan running. Plant alive.**

*"Architect. Operate. Evolve. That's EDEN."*

**Hold. 2 seconds. Then:** *"Thank you."*

**~55 words. ~20 seconds.**

## Q&A READY

**"Show me the KB query live."**
> Trigger a scenario. Agent log shows: query → Syngenta KB response → decision changed.

**"Is this really multi-agent?"**
> "Real Strands SDK agents with real @tool decorators. They disagree — the Resource Agent once overruled the Greenhouse Agent on water allocation. You can see the debate in the log."

**"Does it actually learn?"**
> "After every event: predicted vs actual. Delta exceeds threshold → new flight rule. We can trigger one right now."

**"Why not just one agent?"**
> "One agent collapses to the loudest objective — usually calories. With specialists, the Crew Agent catches what the Resource Agent misses. The disagreement is the value."

**"What about the food psychology?"**
> "NASA classifies fresh food as a psychological countermeasure. In isolated environments, food variety is the #1 habitability factor — ranked above sleep and privacy. EDEN's Crew Agent tracks crew morale, not just macros."

**"How does this use AWS?"**
> "Bedrock with Strands Agents SDK for the AI. AgentCore Gateway for Syngenta KB via MCP. AgentCore Runtime for deployment. The multi-agent debate runs in parallel using ThreadPoolExecutor with real tool calls."

**"Is the plant real?"**
> "Touch it."

## IF THINGS GO WRONG

| Problem | Response |
|---|---|
| WiFi dies | Phone hotspot. Dashboard has cached state. Keep talking. |
| Live demo doesn't respond | "The system is processing — let me show you what happens" → narrate the expected response |
| Sensors don't update | "The agent detected the change internally" → point to agent log |
| LEDs won't dim | Skip silence. "On the dashboard, the system enters resource conservation mode" |
| Over time at 4:30 | Skip Earth bridge details. Go to: "Architect. Operate. Evolve. That's EDEN. Thank you." |
| Brain freeze | Touch the plant. "This plant is alive because an AI is keeping it alive. That's what we built." |
