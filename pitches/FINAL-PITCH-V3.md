# EDEN — Final Pitch V3 (5 minutes)

> Integrates: V2 strengths (proof-first, live demo, Architect, food psychology) + code reality (12 agents, correct names, FLORA plant-voice, real Strands SDK) + ultrabrainstorm (Valentina, emotional beats, Syngenta positioning, cognitive science). Written for spoken delivery. Read it out loud.

- **Valentina** (the basil) on table, LEDs warm white, fan running softly
- Small card: "SOL 280 | EDEN OPERATIONAL" propped against pot
- Dashboard live on screen behind you
- Lars speaks. Bryan drives. Johannes on prop. PJ on backup.

## ACT 1: THE PROOF (0:00–0:30)

*"An AI watered her last night. An AI adjusted her light at 3am. An AI checked her soil moisture 14 minutes ago. No human told it to do any of that."*

**Pause. Let them look at the plant.**

*"That's not a simulation. That's a real plant, real sensors, and a real autonomous agent system running on AWS Bedrock right now. Every decision is logged. Every reading is live."*

**Gesture to dashboard.**

*"We were asked to build an autonomous AI agent to manage a greenhouse on Mars. We didn't just design one. We built one. It's running on this table. We call it EDEN."*

**~85 words. ~30 seconds.**

---

## ACT 2: WHY MARS IS HARD (0:30–1:10)

*"Let's talk about why Mars is hard — and it's not what you think."*

*"We know how to grow plants. Hydroponics, controlled environments, LED lighting — solved problems. Any good team can automate sensor monitoring."*

*"What makes Mars different is one thing: there is no backup. What you grow is what you eat. Every liter of water, every watt of energy cycles through a closed loop. If the system fails, four astronauts have 450 days of freeze-dried monotony — and that's not just uncomfortable. NASA classifies fresh food as a psychological countermeasure, same category as exercise. After 60 days of the same 20 meals, food stops being food. The greenhouse isn't a luxury. It's mental health infrastructure."*

*"So the question isn't 'can we grow plants.' It's: can we build an AI that runs an entire closed-loop food system — monitoring, resource management, stress response, and continuous learning — with zero human intervention, and an understanding that food isn't just fuel? It's how people stay human."*

*"That's what EDEN does."*

**~165 words. ~40 seconds.**

---

## ACT 3: THE SYSTEM (1:10–1:55)

*"EDEN is modeled on the most reliable autonomous system in biology: your immune system. Your skin stops what it can — that's our Flight Rules, deterministic protocols that fire in milliseconds, no AI needed. White blood cells handle what gets through — that's our specialist agents reasoning in real time. And every infection your body survives, it remembers — that's EDEN writing new rules from experience."*

*"Running this system: twelve specialist AI agents operating as a parliament — not a hierarchy, because on Mars with a 22-minute delay, you can't afford a single point of failure."*

**Point to agent panel on dashboard.**

*"SENTINEL monitors threats — radiation, solar events, pressure. AQUA manages the entire resource chain — solar to power to water to crops. VITA tracks crew nutrition and morale — she knows Commander Chen needs iron on Sol 300, and she knows the crew hasn't had fresh greens in three weeks."*

*"And then there's FLORA."*

**Brief pause.**

*"FLORA speaks as the plant. First person. 'I'm flowering. This is my most vulnerable moment. Please protect me.' No other system gives voice to what it's protecting."*

*"Every agent has tools — including access to Syngenta's crop science knowledge base, the same data your agronomists use on Earth. And when the system needs human hands, any agent can call rent-a-human: a formal request to an astronaut with the task, priority, and reasoning."*

*"Every 30 seconds, EDEN checks reality against the plan. If they match, it does nothing. If they diverge, the council convenes."*

**~220 words. ~45 seconds.**

---

## ACT 4: THE LIVE DEMO (1:55–3:00)

*"Now let me show you EDEN doing the hardest thing in the brief: detecting and responding to plant stress. Live. On Valentina."*

**Walk to the plant. Block the light sensor or alter the environment.**

*"I just cut the light. Watch the dashboard."*

**Pause 3-4 seconds. Let sensors update.**

*"SENTINEL detected it instantly. Flight Rules fire first — deterministic, zero latency. Light deficit: compensate with supplemental LED. Under a second."*

**Show flight rule firing on dashboard.**

*"But underneath, the council convenes. AQUA checks: do we have the energy budget? VITA checks: is this crop on a critical nutritional timeline? FLORA says —"*

**Glance at agent log.**

*"— 'I can feel the light dropping. I need it back. I'm mid-growth.' The agents share data, assess tradeoffs, and confirm."*

**3 SECONDS OF SILENCE. Let the dashboard work. Let agents reason.**

*"And tonight, ORACLE — the simulation engine — logs this event and runs hundreds of simulated variants. What if this happens during a dust storm? What if it hits the caloric zone? From those simulations, EDEN writes new flight rules. Next time, the response is faster."*

*"That's the full loop: Observe. Decide. Act. Learn. All four challenge requirements — working, right now, on this table."*

**Restore light. Readings normalize.**

*"Back to nominal. Autonomously."*

**~200 words + pauses. ~65 seconds.**

## ACT 5: PREDICTION + TRIAGE (3:00–3:30)

*"What you just saw was reactive. But EDEN also looks ahead."*

*"Right now, SENTINEL is tracking live NASA solar data. When a coronal mass ejection is detected — and this uses real DONKI data — EDEN calculates the transit time. 50 hours at 1,247 km/s. And it starts preparing."*

*"ORACLE runs 100 simulations. Standard protocol: shut down and wait, 40% crop loss. EDEN's pre-emptive protocol: 3%."*

*"But here's what costs the system. FLORA in the herb zone says: 'I'm the basil. Please don't cut me.' And VITA says: 'Commander Chen requested basil three times this week.'"*

**Beat.**

*"The system remembered what she wanted. And it let it die anyway. Because the crew needs vitamin C more than variety."*

*"EDEN doesn't hide that cost. The triage log shows every sacrifice and its human consequence. That's not just engineering. That's ethics."*

**~145 words. ~30 seconds.**

---

## ACT 6: THE ARCHITECT (3:30–3:55)

*"Every team today will show you a system that runs a greenhouse. EDEN does something none of them do: it designs the greenhouse first."*

*"Before a single seed is planted, give EDEN your constraints: four astronauts, 450 days, cargo mass, seed inventory, Mars conditions. EDEN outputs a complete design — which crops, how many, which zones, a full 450-day planting schedule, and projected nutritional coverage for every crew member, every week."*

**Flash the Architect output on dashboard.**

*"It plans backward from human needs. It knows spinach goes in the ground on Sol 250 so there's iron available on Sol 300. Every other agricultural AI starts with a farm that already exists. EDEN starts with constraints and creates the farm."*

**~120 words. ~25 seconds.**

---

## ACT 7: THE EARTH BRIDGE (3:55–4:35)

*"We built EDEN for Mars because Mars is the ultimate test: zero redundancy, no margin for error."*

*"But that description is starting to apply to Earth."*

*"Aquifers depleting. Soil degrading. Climate making every growing season less predictable."*

*"Imagine Grace — a smallholder farmer in Nakuru, Kenya. Two hectares. Family of six. Seasonal rainfall that didn't come. She's making the same decisions EDEN just made: what to save, what to sacrifice. She's making them alone."*

*"Same crop science from Syngenta's knowledge base. Same decision architecture. Same closed loop."*

*"Syngenta's Cropwise tells farmers their field has a problem. EDEN is the next step — it tells itself what to do about it, and does it. Autonomous advisory, at the moment it matters, where no agronomist can reach."*

*"Mars forced us to build the hardest version. The Earth version is easier."*

**~145 words. ~40 seconds.**

## ACT 8: THE CLOSE (4:35–5:00)

*"We were asked to build an autonomous AI agent system to manage a Martian greenhouse."*

*"We built one that designs it, runs it, and teaches itself."*

*"We proved it on the hardest farm in the solar system."*

**Look at the judges. Not the screen.**

*"And we proved it on Valentina."*

**Gesture to the basil. LEDs full warm white. Fan running. Plant alive.**

*"Whoever built Syngenta's crop science knowledge base — the people who encoded wheat stress tolerance and lettuce disease thresholds and nutritional models — their work just kept four astronauts fed for 450 days."*

**Half-step toward judges. Direct eye contact. Center judge.**

*"What if the first farm on Mars was designed by an AI that learned from Syngenta?"*

**Hold. 3 seconds of silence. Do not speak. Do not move.**

*Then, softly:* "Thank you."

**~115 words. ~25 seconds.**

## WHAT CHANGED FROM V2

| V2 | V3 | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Five AI agents" with wrong names (MARS, CREW, GREENHOUSE, RESOURCE, ORCHESTRATOR) | "Twelve specialist agents" with correct code names (SENTINEL, AQUA, VITA, FLORA, ORACLE) | Dashboard shows the real names. Mismatch kills credibility. 12 > 5 for judge impression. |
| No FLORA plant-voice | FLORA speaks: "I'm flowering. This is my most vulnerable moment." | Most creative element in the codebase. No other team has AI speaking as the plant. |
| No plant name | "Valentina" (Valentina Tereshkova, first woman in space) | Endowment effect — judges own her by the end. |
| K8s metaphor in one line | Immune system metaphor: "Skin = flight rules, white blood cells = agents, immunological memory = learning" | Every judge has an immune system. Zero explanation needed. |
| No reconciler mention | "Every 30 seconds, EDEN checks reality against the plan." | The actual innovation — one sentence communicates the architecture better than any diagram. |
| No emotional triage moment | "The system remembered what she wanted. And it let it die anyway." | The heart beat. Three missing frequencies: tenderness, humility, recognition. |
| Ends with "Thank you" | Ends with open question + 3s silence: "What if the first farm on Mars was designed by an AI that learned from Syngenta?" | Zeigarnik effect — open loop persists into deliberation. |
| No Syngenta product positioning | "Cropwise tells farmers their field has a problem. EDEN tells itself what to do about it." | Speaks Syngenta's own language. Positions EDEN as Cropwise 2.0. |
| No KB identity activation | "Whoever built Syngenta's crop science knowledge base — their work just kept four astronauts fed." | If ANY judge contributed to the KB, their life's work is now heroic. |
| No pre-pitch basil crush | Optional: crush basil leaves before starting. | Olfactory memory bypasses thalamus. Strongest memory pathway. |
| Generic "fresh food matters" | NASA countermeasure + "food is how people stay human" | Backed by deep research. Validates crop diversity as necessity. |

## Q&A READY

**"Show me the KB query live."**
> Trigger scenario. Agent log: query → Syngenta KB response → decision changed. "Without this data, EDEN protects potatoes. The KB changes the decision to wheat."

**"Is this really multi-agent?"**
> "Twelve real Strands SDK agents with real @tool decorators running in parallel via ThreadPoolExecutor. Three-round deliberation: agents disagree BY NAME. 'I disagree with CHRONOS because...' We also have PATHFINDER the mycologist and HESTIA the crew psychologist tracking morale."

**"Does it actually learn?"**
> "Real learning pipeline in code. After every event: predicted vs actual. When delta exceeds threshold, a new rule is proposed. After 3 cycles without conflict, it's promoted to active. We can trigger one right now."

**"Why multiple agents?"**
> "One agent collapses to the loudest objective — usually calories. With twelve specialists, VITA catches nutritional gaps AQUA would trade away. HESTIA catches morale impacts SENTINEL would ignore. The disagreement IS the value."

**"What about the food psychology?"**
> "NASA classifies fresh food as a countermeasure. Stuster's research across submarines, Antarctic stations, and ISS: food is the #1 habitability factor — above sleep, above privacy. EDEN's HESTIA agent tracks morale, food preferences, and plans surprise harvests."

**"How does this use AWS?"**
> "Bedrock with Claude via Strands Agents SDK. AgentCore Gateway for Syngenta KB via MCP. Real tool calls — agents call read_sensors(), run_simulation(), query_syngenta_kb() as actual Strands @tool functions. Twelve agents run in parallel."

**"Is the plant real?"**
> "Touch her."

**"What would you build next?"**
