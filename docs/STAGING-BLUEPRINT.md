# EDEN Pitch: Staging Blueprint

**Duration:** 3 minutes (180 seconds)
**Presenter:** Lars (single voice, start to finish)
**Supporting cast:** Bryan, PJ, Johannes (silent roles + Q&A)
**Stage elements:** Living basil plant with LED strip + fan (on Raspberry Pi), projected dashboard, SOL card prop

---

## THE PHILOSOPHY

This is not a presentation. It is a piece of three-act chamber theater where the plant is the protagonist and Lars is the narrator. The technology exists to serve the plant's survival. Every sensory channel you control -- LED color, fan sound, screen brightness, physical movement, silence -- is part of your theatrical rig.

Three moments define this pitch. Everything else is connective tissue between them:

1. **THE INTRODUCTION** (0:25) -- Lars gestures to the plant. The judges meet the protagonist.
2. **THE SILENCE** (1:35) -- LEDs dim red. Fan stops. Lars does not speak for 3 seconds. This is the moment judges will remember tomorrow.
3. **THE CLOSING LINE** (2:54) -- Direct eye contact. "Because on Mars, there is no second chance."

If you rehearse nothing else, rehearse these three.

**Key positioning rules:**
- The plant sits at the FRONT EDGE of the table, closest to judges. Not center-back. Front-center. If the table is 60cm deep, the plant is at the 10cm mark. Judges should be able to see the LED glow without leaning.
- Lars stands to the LEFT of the table (from the audience's perspective). This creates a visual triangle: Lars (left) -- Plant (center) -- Screen (right/behind). Lars can gesture toward either without turning his back.
- The laptop running the dashboard sits on Bryan's side of the table, connected to the projector. Bryan can reach it for emergency input without crossing the stage.
- The SOL card (small, weathered, handwritten: "SOL 247 | EDEN OPERATIONAL") is propped against the plant pot. A detail that says "this world is real." Judges who notice it are rewarded; those who don't miss nothing.
- Johannes, PJ, and Bryan stand BEHIND the table in a relaxed line. Not rigid. Arms at sides or loosely clasped. They are the crew -- present, calm, professional.

**Judge sightlines:** From a seated position, judges see: Lars in front-left, the glowing plant at front-center of the table (at their eye level or slightly above), and the projected dashboard on the wall behind. The plant and the presenter are both in the lower visual field (where people naturally look). The dashboard is peripheral backdrop.

## PRE-PITCH SETUP (-30 to 0:00)

This is free storytelling time. Judges watch you set up. Use it.

**What the audience sees:** While the previous team packs up, your team walks to the stage. Johannes carries the plant -- carefully, like something alive, because it is. Lars adjusts the position on the table. PJ connects the Pi. The LEDs come on warm white. The fan starts humming.

**The casual introduction:** Before the clock starts, Lars turns to the judges conversationally (not performing -- just talking to humans): "This is basil. It's real. It's been growing for three weeks." Then turns back to the setup. This is not part of the pitch. It is the most important sentence of the entire presentation, because it makes every subsequent moment land harder. When the LEDs dim later, the judges are not thinking "is that a real plant?" -- they are thinking "oh no, the basil."

**The SOL card:** Johannes props it against the pot during setup. If a judge notices it and reads it, they are already inside the world.

**LED state:** Warm white, steady glow.
**Fan state:** On. Humming softly. This ambient sound becomes the baseline that makes its later absence felt.
**Dashboard state:** Dark Mars surface. Sol counter beginning. Atmospheric. Not demanding attention.

**Team readiness positions:** All four standing behind table. Lars moves to his left-of-table position 5 seconds before the pitch clock starts.

## ACT 1: "THE LONELIEST FARMER" (0:00-0:45)

**Purpose:** Establish the world. Introduce the plant as character.
**Emotional beat:** Curiosity leads to understanding.

### Blocking + Script

**0:00-0:10 | THE OPENING**
- Lars faces judges directly. Not the screen. Not the plant. The judges.
- Eye contact: center judge first, then sweep.
- Feet planted. No movement yet. Stillness at the start = authority.

> "Four astronauts. 450 days on Mars. 22-minute signal delay. They can't bring a farmer. So we built one."

- Pace: deliberate. Each sentence is its own beat. Do not rush.
- LED state: warm white. Fan: on. Dashboard: Sol counter ticking, zones green.

**0:10-0:25 | WHAT EDEN DOES**
- Lars stays in position. Brief glance at screen (1 second) when mentioning the dashboard, then back to judges.
- Dashboard shows: nutritional tracking, 5 green zones, agent log beginning to populate.

> "EDEN is an AI that manages every system in the greenhouse. Water, light, nutrients, atmosphere. On Sol 1, it already planned every harvest through Sol 450."

**0:25-0:27 | THE INTRODUCTION (Key Moment 1)**

This is where the judges meet the protagonist.

- Lars takes ONE step toward the plant.
- Extends his LEFT hand toward it -- palm UP, waist height, fingers relaxed. An offering, not a presentation. Like introducing someone at a dinner party, not revealing a prize.
- Holds the gesture for 2 full seconds while saying:

> "So we built one."

[The repetition of this phrase, now with the gesture toward a living plant, recontextualizes it. The first time it was abstract. Now it's concrete. That plant IS the farmer.]

- **REHEARSAL NOTE:** Practice this gesture 20 times. If the palm is too high, it looks like a game show. If it's too stiff, it looks rehearsed. Waist height. Open. Relaxed. You are introducing a friend.

**0:27-0:35 | LET THE PLANT BE**
- Lars steps back to left position. Does NOT keep gesturing at the plant.
- The plant is now in the judges' awareness. They will look at it on their own. Let them.

**0:35-0:45 | NOMINAL OPERATIONS**
- Dashboard shows agent log: SENTINEL detecting something.
- Lars glances at screen for 1 second, then back to judges.

> "Right now, it just caught a fungal disease risk that won't show symptoms for three days. EDEN doesn't just react to crises. It prevents them."

[Do NOT say "VPD drift" or "Botrytis." Translate the technical into human consequence. The judge doesn't need to understand the mechanism. They need to understand the capability.]

> "But let's see what happens when a real one comes."

- LED state: warm white. Fan: on. Dashboard: green tint. Everything is healthy.

**Team behavior during Act 1:** Still. Watching Lars. When Lars gestures to the plant at 0:25, all three team members shift their gaze to the plant for 2 seconds, then back to Lars. This synchronized attention tells the judges "look there" without saying it.

## ACT 2: "EDEN SEES THE FUTURE" (0:45-1:20)

**Purpose:** Introduce the threat. Show the system's intelligence.
**Emotional beat:** Understanding becomes tension.

### Blocking + Script

**0:45-0:50 | URGENCY SHIFT**
- Lars takes a half-step FORWARD (toward judges). Not toward the screen or plant -- toward the people. This closes distance and signals: something's changing.
- Voice drops slightly lower in pitch. Pace increases by 10%. Not rushing -- urgent.
- LED state: still warm white. Fan: still on. The calm before.

**0:50-0:55 | THE CME ALERT (Dashboard Moment)**

- Dashboard: FULL-SCREEN RED FLASH for 1 second, then CME ALERT banner with countdown 50:42:17.
- This should be IMPOSSIBLE TO MISS on the projected screen. Not a subtle notification -- an alarm.
- Lars does NOT turn to look at the screen. He PAUSES for 1.5 seconds. Lets the judges see the alert. Then:

> "A real coronal mass ejection. NASA data, happening right now. Speed: 1,247 kilometers per second. Mars impact: 50 hours."

[The fact that Lars doesn't look at the screen says: "I knew. The system knew." This is authority.]

- **VERBAL CUE for dashboard:** Lars says "But let's see what happens when a real one comes" at the end of Act 1. This is the trigger for Bryan to advance the dashboard to the CME alert state. Bryan counts to 5 (during Lars's urgency shift lines) then triggers the alert.

**0:55-1:10 | THE SYSTEM RESPONDS**
- Dashboard background tint shifts subtly from green to AMBER. Not announced. Not pointed at. Judges feel the warmth without knowing why.

> "In seconds, EDEN's threat detector queries the Syngenta knowledge base. Wheat is flowering -- the most vulnerable stage. Without that data, the system would protect potatoes first. The crop science changes the decision."

> "Three strategies are simulated. One loses 40% of the crop. One loses 12%. One loses 3%."

- Lars gestures briefly to screen (2 seconds) as dashboard shows strategy comparison. Then returns gaze to judges.

> "EDEN chooses the strategy that saves the most nutrition for the crew."

**1:10-1:20 | TRANSITION TO CRISIS**
- Lars begins moving toward CENTER -- toward the plant. Slow. One step every 3 seconds.
- Voice: the pace slows back down. We are approaching the climax. Deceleration signals gravity.

> "Now it has 50 hours. And it starts preparing."

- Dashboard: water gauge begins climbing. Desalination at MAX. Battery charging.
- LED state: shifts from warm white to AMBER. This is the first visible change on the plant. Subtle. A warning color.

**Team behavior during Act 2:** Johannes subtly adjusts something on the Pi -- a micro-gesture of authenticity. Not scripted to be noticed, but adds texture. Bryan is near the laptop, having triggered the CME alert.

---

## ACT 3: "PREPARING FOR THE STORM" (1:20-2:00)

**Purpose:** THE CLIMAX. The plant is in danger. The system makes life-and-death choices.
**Emotional beat:** Tension peaks. Held breath. The unspoken.

### Blocking + Script

**1:20-1:30 | STANDING WITH THE PLANT**
- Lars arrives at CENTER -- directly beside the plant. This is the ONLY time during the pitch that the presenter stands WITH the plant. They are in it together.
- One hand rests near (not on) the pot. Physical proximity = emotional connection.

> "Water reserves are climbing. Battery at maximum. The system pre-harvests the spinach to lock in nutrition. It stress-hardens the wheat -- adjusting nutrients based on Syngenta's crop science."

- Dashboard: triage panel appears. Water gauge still climbing. Background tint deepening toward red.

**1:30-1:32 | THE TRIGGER**
- Lars looks at the plant. Voice drops to near-conversational:

> "And then the storm hits."

[VERBAL CUE: "the storm hits" is the trigger for the LED transition and fan shutoff. The team member controlling the Pi (Johannes) initiates the sequence on this phrase.]

- **2-second pause.** Lars says nothing. Lets the words hang.

**1:32-1:35 | THE DIMMING (Key Moment 2 -- Setup)**
- LEDs shift from amber toward dim red over 3 seconds.
- Lars says NOTHING. He watches the plant WITH the judges.
- This is the unspoken moment. The audience sees the light changing on a living thing. Their brain registers it before their conscious mind processes it: "the plant --"

**1:35 | THE SILENCE (Key Moment 2 -- Climax)**
- LEDs reach dim red.
- Fan motor winds down and STOPS.
- Dashboard background: deep red.
- Lars is still. The room is quiet.

**1:35-1:38 | THREE SECONDS OF NOTHING**

Count internally: one Mississippi, two Mississippi, three Mississippi.

[This silence -- after 95 seconds of continuous voice + fan hum -- is the most memorable sensory moment of the pitch. The fan's absence creates a felt void. The dim red light on a living plant creates visual urgency. The presenter's stillness creates human weight. This is where you win.

DO NOT FILL THIS SILENCE. It will feel like an eternity. It will feel like something is wrong. It is not wrong. It is the moment 41 other teams did not have the nerve to create.

Mark this in every version of the script: **[THREE MISSISSIPPI -- DO NOT SPEAK]**]

**1:38-1:45 | BREAKING THE SILENCE**
- Lars breaks the silence. Voice is QUIETER than before. Not projecting. Almost private.

> "EDEN chose to save the wheat. It let the spinach go. Because the crew needs vitamin C more than they need variety."

- Pause. One beat.

> "And it made that choice alone. 227 million kilometers from the nearest human expert."

**1:45-1:50 | THE LEARNING**
- Lars looks at the plant for 1 second, then back to judges.

> "After the storm, EDEN compared what it predicted to what actually happened. The loss was 4%, not the 3% it planned for. So it wrote a new rule: next time, start stockpiling two hours earlier. Sol 1, it had 50 rules. By Sol 450, it has 300. Every crisis makes it smarter."

**1:50-2:00 | THE RECOVERY BEGINS**
- LEDs begin warming: dim red slowly returning toward amber, then toward warm white. This transition takes the full remaining time through the end of Act 4.
- Fan turns back ON at 1:55. The return of the hum = normalcy restored.
- Dashboard: background shifting from red toward warm tones. Post-storm debrief visible. Flight rule count: 54 leads to 55.

> "The storm passes. The system recovers. And the crew eats fresh food tonight."

- Lars begins stepping back from center toward his LEFT position. The crisis is over. Space returns between the presenter and the plant.

**BACKUP PLAN -- Fan not audible:**
If the fan is too quiet for judges to notice the silence, Lars adds one word after the fan stops at 1:35: "Listen." Then holds the 3 seconds. The word directs attention to the absence.

**BACKUP PLAN -- LEDs not visible:**
If venue lighting washes out the LEDs, the verbal narration carries the climax entirely. The silence and the stillness work regardless. Optionally, Lars can PICK UP the plant and hold it at chest height from 1:30-1:45, bringing the LEDs to eye level 1-2 meters from judges. This is dramatic but risky -- only do it if rehearsed thoroughly and the plant is light enough to hold steadily.

---

## ACT 4: "THE MIRROR" (2:00-2:30)

**Purpose:** Release + expand. Mars to Earth. Syngenta connection.
**Emotional beat:** Resolution opens into expansion.

### Blocking + Script

**2:00-2:15 | THE PIVOT**
- Lars is back at his LEFT position. Body language opens -- shoulders back, hands unclenched. The crisis is behind us.
- LEDs are warming back toward full white. Fan is humming again. Dashboard is recovering.
- Lars faces judges directly. No screen glances. This section is pure human-to-human.

> "Everything you just saw works on Earth too."

- Pause. One beat. Let them reframe.

> "Syngenta has one agronomist for every 5,000 farmers in Sub-Saharan Africa. Picture a maize farmer in Kenya. The rains are late. Her nearest expert is 200 kilometers away. She's making the same decisions EDEN just made -- what to save, what to sacrifice -- but she's making them alone."

[One person, not a statistic. "800 million" is a number. The Kenya farmer is a human. Judges remember humans.]

**2:15-2:30 | THE CONNECTION**

> "Same Syngenta crop science. Same decision architecture. Same knowledge base. Mars forced us to build the hardest version. The Earth version is easier."

- LED state: returning to full warm white. Plant is healthy again. Glowing.
- Dashboard: all zones green. Sol counter still ticking. Life continues.

---

## CLOSE (2:30-3:00)

**Purpose:** Conviction. The audience leaves certain.
**Emotional beat:** Expansion narrows to a single, unshakable statement.

### Blocking + Script

**2:30-2:50 | THE LIST (Building Rhythm)**
- Lars stands still. CENTER position now -- he's moved slightly right from his Act 4 position. Feet planted. No fidgeting. Stillness is conviction.
- Plant is beside him, full warm white glow. Fan humming. Everything alive.

> "We built the loneliest farmer in the solar system."

- Beat.

> "It plans every harvest."

- Beat.

> "It detects disease before symptoms appear."

- Beat.

> "It sees storms 50 hours ahead."

- Beat.

> "It learns from every decision."

[Each line is its own breath. The rhythm creates expectation. The pace is measured -- one capability per beat. This is a crescendo built on repetition.]

**2:50-2:52 | THE BREAK**
- Lars glances at the plant for 1 second. A quiet acknowledgment -- like checking on a friend.
- Then back to judges.

> "And it gets better every day."

**2:52-2:54 | THE HALF-STEP**
- Lars takes one half-step forward. Toward the judges. Into their space.
- This is the physical signal that the next line is different.

**2:54-2:57 | THE CLOSING LINE (Key Moment 3)**

Full stop. Direct eye contact. CENTER JUDGE. Not scanning the room -- one person.

Voice drops to conversational volume. Lars is not projecting to a room. He is speaking TO one person.

> "Because on Mars, there is no second chance."

**2:57-3:00 | HOLD**

Do not speak. Do not move. Do not break eye contact for 3 seconds.

Then: soft "Thank you." Small nod. One step back.

[The silence after the closing line is as important as the silence at the climax. The first silence was about danger. This silence is about conviction. Both work the same way: they give the audience's brain time to encode what just happened.]

## SOUND DESIGN

**The fan is your sound rig.** It has two states -- on and off -- and the transition between them is your single most powerful audio tool.

| Time | Fan State | Effect |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Setup through 1:34 | ON (humming) | Establishes baseline ambient. Judges habituate to it. They stop noticing it consciously. |
| 1:35 (on "the storm hits" + 3s) | OFF | The ABSENCE is the signal. The room feels different. Something is wrong. This is sub-cognitive -- judges feel it before they understand it. |
| 1:55 | ON | Normalcy returns. Relief. The hum that was invisible before is now comforting. |

**Loudness requirement:** The fan needs to be audible from 2-3 meters (judge distance). Test this in advance. If the fan is too quiet, consider mounting it elevated (on a small box behind the plant) or using a slightly larger fan. The fan sound must be FELT, even if only subliminally.

**Backup:** If the fan is inaudible in the venue, Lars adds "Listen" at 1:35 and pauses. The verbal cue directs attention to the silence even if the sonic contrast is subtle.

## PHYSICAL PROPS

### The SOL Card
- Small card (index card size or slightly smaller), slightly weathered/aged appearance
- Handwritten in clean block letters: "SOL 247 | EDEN OPERATIONAL"
- Propped against the base of the plant pot, facing judges
- Purpose: a detail that rewards attention. Judges who notice it are already inside the world. Judges who don't miss nothing critical.
- Do NOT mention it verbally. It exists for judges who lean in.

### How to make it look intentional, not amateur:
- Use slightly off-white or kraft paper, not bright white printer paper
- Block letters in dark pen, not cursive, not typed
- Slightly smaller than an index card -- the constraint makes it feel designed
- Optional: a thin red border or a small EDEN logo in the corner

### What NOT to do:
- No printed labels (looks corporate, not authentic)
- No multiple props (one card is a detail; three cards is a craft fair)
- No props that need explanation (if you have to say "as you can see on this card," the prop failed)

## Q&A PROTOCOL (After the pitch)

### Physical Protocol
Team is lined up behind the table (from judges' left to right): Lars, Bryan, PJ, Johannes.

When a judge asks a question:
1. **Lars takes first question by default.** He is already in speaking mode. First question should feel like a natural continuation.
2. **For domain-specific questions:** The relevant team member takes ONE HALF-STEP FORWARD. This is the physical signal -- no verbal negotiation ("uh, Johannes, you want to take this one?"). Half-step forward = "this is mine."
3. **Domain routing:**
   - Agent system, AI, software architecture --> Bryan
   - Data, crop science, Syngenta KB, NASA data --> PJ
   - Hardware, sensors, Raspberry Pi, physical demo --> Johannes
   - Everything else, strategy, vision --> Lars
4. **If unclear whose question it is:** Lars takes it.
5. **After answering:** Step back to original position.

### Tone Shift
- Pitch mode = performance (one voice, theatrical timing)
- Q&A mode = conversation (warm, collaborative, all four contributing)
- Lars signals this shift by relaxing his posture after "Thank you" and breaking into a natural smile. The formality is over. Now it's a conversation.

### The Plant During Q&A
- Leave it glowing warm white with the fan on
- If a judge walks up and touches it or asks about it, invite them: "It's real basil. Three weeks old. The LEDs and fan are controlled by the Raspberry Pi right there."
- Let the plant be a conversation starter, not a prop to protect

## CUE SHEET (Technical Triggers)

All tech transitions are triggered by VERBAL CUES from Lars, not by timestamps. This prevents desync if Lars speeds up or slows down.

| Lars Says | Who Acts | What Happens | Backup |
|-----------|----------|-------------|--------|
| "when a real one comes" (end of Act 1) | Bryan | Count 5 seconds, trigger CME alert on dashboard | If Bryan misses the cue, Lars pauses an extra beat at 0:50 and says "And right now--" as a secondary cue |
| Lars starts moving toward plant (~1:10) | Johannes | Begin LED shift: warm white to amber (slow, 15 seconds) | If LEDs don't shift, Lars continues narration unaffected |
| "the storm hits" | Johannes | LED shift: amber to dim red (fast, 3 seconds) + fan OFF | If fan/LED fail, Lars adds "Listen" and holds the pause anyway. The silence from his voice still works. |
| "the storm passes" | Johannes | LED shift: dim red to amber to warm white (slow, 20 seconds) + fan ON | If recovery doesn't trigger, Lars carries on -- the verbal arc resolves regardless |

**Manual backup:** Johannes has a phone or keyboard mapped to LED/fan commands. If he misses a verbal cue, he can trigger manually within 3-5 seconds. A 3-second delay on the LED dim after "the storm hits" is fine -- it reads as the storm arriving, not as a tech failure.

**Total failure plan:** If ALL tech fails (Pi crashes, LEDs die, fan is unplugged), the pitch works anyway. Lars never says "as you can see, the LEDs are dimming." He narrates the story independently. The tech enhances; it does not carry.

## THE PLANT'S ARC (Protagonist Journey)

The basil is not a prop. It is a character with a three-act arc:

**BEGINNING (Setup through Act 1):** Healthy. Warm white glow. Fan humming = breathing. Alive and thriving. The judges were told "this is basil, it's real" during setup. It sits at the front of the table, glowing like a small beacon. It is doing fine. Nobody is worried about it.

**MIDDLE (Act 2 through Act 3 climax):** Threatened. The LEDs shift from warm white to amber to dim red. The fan -- its breath -- stops. The light on its leaves goes from "life" to "survival." The presenter stands beside it, narrating the AI's decisions about what lives and what dies. The plant cannot speak, cannot move, cannot defend itself. It depends entirely on the system the team built. This is the emotional core: a living thing in danger, and an AI making decisions about its fate.

**END (Act 4 through Close):** Recovered. LEDs warm back to white. Fan resumes. The plant is healthy again because the system worked. When Lars glances at it during the closing list ("it gets better every day"), it is a quiet acknowledgment: you survived. We built something that kept you alive.

The arc mirrors the pitch's thesis: autonomous intelligence protecting life when no human expert is available.
